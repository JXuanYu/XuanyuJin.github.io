Leonid Keselman

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Artificial Intelligence at the School of Computer and Artificial Intelligence, Southwest Jiaotong University. My advisor is Hongmei Chen.

From 2022 to 2025, I studied at at the School of Computer and Artificial Intelligence, Southwest Jiaotong University. I conduct research on evolutionary algorithms and have a paper currently in the process of publication. As the team leader, I am leading a research team in the 2024 National Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program. Our project, Assessment of Reading Ability and Early Screening of Dyslexia Based on Large Models , has been rated at the national level and has resulted in relevant patent applications (currently under review).

I am conducting research on evolutionary algorithms and currently have a paper under review for publication. As the team leader, I am participating in the 2024 National Undergraduate Innovation and Entrepreneurship Training Program under the supervision of Prof. Xiangzhi Meng from Peking University and Prof. Jielei Chu from Southwest Jiaotong University. Our project, titled "Research on Reading Ability Assessment and Early Screening of Reading Disabilities Based on Large Models," has been rated as a national-level project and has resulted in a related patent (currently under review).

During the 2024-2025 period, I am engaged in a remote research internship at the AIMING Lab, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, under the supervision of Prof. Huaxiu Yao, focusing on AI alignment. I am contributing to multiple projects, with two co-authored papers (MJ-Video and GLIMPSE) currently under review.

Previously, during my sophomore year, I interned at CIDI, an intelligent driving company, where I conducted research on the role of large models in autonomous driving under the mentorship of Dr. Zhaorun Chen from the University of Chicago.
